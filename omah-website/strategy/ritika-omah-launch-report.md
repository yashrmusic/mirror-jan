## 1. Executive Recommendation

Omah should not try to become a broad wellness store in the first phase. The best business path is to build a high-margin, low-logistics, content-friendly ritual commerce brand.

The first 90 days should focus on three profitable lanes:

1. Wearable ritual jewellery: bracelets, charm necklaces, malas, anklets, rings.
2. Scent and home ritual products: incense, room sprays, candles, oils, wax melts.
3. Ritual gift kits: bundled sets for desk reset, sleep, protection, new home, birthdays, and corporate gifting.

Supporting products like ritual cards, journals, pouches, trays, and inserts should be used to raise perceived value and bundle pricing. They are not the hero category, but they improve margins.

Phase 2 can add beauty-lite personal care, such as lip balm, pulse balm, perfume oils, body oils, and bath salts. Phase 3 can add functional teas and consumables, but only after packaging, sourcing, compliance, and repeat-purchase systems are clearer.

## 2. Why These Categories

The business filter is simple:

- High gross margin
- Low shipping weight
- Low breakage risk
- Easy to shoot on Instagram
- Giftable
- Bundle-friendly
- Not heavily regulated
- Can be sourced in small batches
- Fits Omah without confusing the brand

### Category 1: Wearable Ritual Jewellery

Examples:

- Evil eye bracelets
- Pyrite bracelets
- Citrine / green aventurine bracelets
- Minimal necklaces
- Charms
- Rudraksha-inspired pieces
- Birthstone-style pieces
- Anklets and rings

Why it works:

- Very low shipping cost.
- Easy to store.
- High perceived value versus landed cost.
- Works well on human models and UGC.
- Strong gifting potential.
- Can be sold as singles, stacks, and kits.

Business role:

This should be Omah's first customer acquisition category. People can understand it quickly and creators can make content easily.

### Category 2: Scent and Home Ritual

Examples:

- Incense cones
- Room sprays
- Essential oil blends
- Scented candles
- Wax melts
- Car fragrance hangers
- Ceramic or wooden holders

Why it works:

- Repeat-purchase potential.
- Easy emotional language: calm, focus, sleep, reset, protection.
- Strong bundle economics.
- Strong gifting use cases.
- Good fit with Indian homes, offices, salons, and bedrooms.

Market signal:

Grand View Research estimates India's home fragrance market generated USD 446.6 million in 2025 and projects it to reach USD 1,045.6 million by 2033, with an expected CAGR of 11.1% from 2026 to 2033.

Business role:

This should become Omah's repeat-purchase engine after jewellery brings in first customers.

### Category 3: Ritual Gift Kits

Examples:

- Begin Within Kit
- Desk Reset Kit
- Night Ritual Kit
- New Home Kit
- Protection Kit
- Bridesmaid Calm Box
- Corporate Desk Reset Kit

Why it works:

- Bundles increase average order value.
- Packaging raises perceived value.
- Customers buy gifts with less price resistance.
- Kits let us combine jewellery + scent + card + tray into one stronger offer.
- Good for festivals, birthdays, housewarming, weddings, and corporate gifting.

Market signal:

IMARC estimates the India gifting market reached USD 816.3 million in 2025 and projects it to reach USD 1,089.9 million by 2034.

Business role:

This should become Omah's profit and AOV engine. Single bracelets may bring traffic, but kits create better money.

### Category 4: Beauty-Lite Personal Care

Examples:

- Lip balm
- Pulse-point balm
- Perfume oils
- Body oils
- Bath salts
- Hair oil
- Roll-ons

Why it works:

- High repeat potential.
- Small and easy to ship.
- Strong premium packaging opportunity.
- Fits Omah if positioned as ritual care, not clinical skincare.

Market signal:

IMARC estimates India beauty and personal care was valued at USD 31.19 billion in 2025 and is projected to reach USD 48.72 billion by 2034.

Business role:

This should be Phase 2. It is attractive, but we should avoid competing directly with skincare brands too early.

### Category 5: Functional Teas and Calm Drinks

Examples:

- Tulsi calm tea
- Chamomile sleep tea
- Rose tea
- Blue pea tea
- Digestion tea
- Focus tea

Why it works:

- Consumable and repeatable.
- Easy to bundle with night rituals and desk rituals.
- Fits Indian botanical wellness.

Business caution:

Food products need stronger compliance, taste testing, shelf-life checks, and vendor reliability. This is not the easiest first category.

Business role:

Phase 3, after brand trust and fulfilment are stable.

## 3. What We Should Avoid Early

Avoid these categories in the first 90 days:

- Supplements and gummies: regulatory and claims risk.
- Serious skincare: very crowded, expensive, trust-heavy.
- Electronics or diffusers: warranty and defect risk.
- Apparel: sizing, returns, inventory complexity.
- Heavy decor: shipping damage and storage pain.
- Generic fashion jewellery without ritual meaning: too easy to compare on price.

The early Omah rule should be: small, premium-feeling, easy to ship, easy to gift, easy to film.

## 5. Budget Needed

### Lean 45-Day Launch: Rs. 1.5L to Rs. 2.5L

This is the minimum serious launch.

- Product samples: Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 45,000
- Packaging samples: Rs. 20,000 to Rs. 40,000
- First micro inventory: Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 90,000
- Website/Shopify setup: Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 25,000
- UGC/content: Rs. 20,000 to Rs. 50,000
- Ads test budget: Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 50,000
- Tools/miscellaneous: Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 20,000

Best when: we want to validate demand before stocking heavily.

### Serious 60-Day Launch: Rs. 4L to Rs. 7L

This is the recommended range if we want a real shot.

- Product samples and vendor tests: Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 80,000
- Packaging and inserts: Rs. 60,000 to Rs. 1.2L
- First inventory: Rs. 1.5L to Rs. 3L
- Shopify, domain, apps, tracking: Rs. 40,000 to Rs. 80,000
- Creator videos and product shoot: Rs. 75,000 to Rs. 1.5L
- Paid ads testing: Rs. 75,000 to Rs. 1.5L
- Buffer: Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 1L

Best when: we want to launch with enough content, inventory, and testing power.

### Aggressive 90-Day Launch: Rs. 10L to Rs. 15L

This is only useful if we already have strong founder bandwidth and supplier confidence.

- Deeper inventory
- Better packaging
- Professional shoots
- Multiple creator batches
- Meta ads testing
- Influencer seeding
- WhatsApp automation
- PR and gifting

Recommendation: do not start here unless the first product tests show strong pull.

## 6. Time Needed

### Realistic Launch Timeline

Minimum: 45 days  
Recommended: 60 days  
Strong launch: 90 days

### 8-Week Launch Plan

Week 1: Decide the launch lanes  
Finalize the first 12 SKUs: 5 wearables, 4 scent/home products, 3 gift kits.

Week 2: Supplier and sample sprint  
Order samples from 3-5 vendors per product type. Compare quality, cost, MOQ, delivery time, and packaging.

Week 3: Packaging and offer design  
Finalize boxes, pouches, inserts, ritual cards, bundle names, and price ladder.

Week 4: Product pages and visual system  
Build Shopify product pages, shoot product images, create 10-15 AI/product lifestyle visuals.

Week 5: UGC and creator batch  
Get 5-10 creator-style videos made. Test hooks around gifting, desk reset, night routine, and daily wear.

Week 6: Soft launch  
Open waitlist, WhatsApp interest capture, Instagram teaser grid, and first bundle previews.

Week 7: First drop launch  
Launch limited stock. Push 3 hero bundles and 3 low-ticket entry SKUs.

Week 8: Cut weak products  
Double down on products with saves, DMs, add-to-carts, and first orders. Remove weak SKUs quickly.

## 7. Marketing Plan

### Core Positioning

Omah should own:

"Modern Indian ritual goods for the way you live now."

Do not position as:

- Religious store
- Generic jewellery store
- Generic wellness store
- Healing claims brand
- Astrology-heavy brand

Use language like:

- Wear it close.
- Place it where your day begins.
- Gift a softer reset.
- Begin within.

### Instagram Content Engine

Weekly minimum:

- 5 UGC-style videos
- 3 product demonstrations
- 2 aesthetic lifestyle reels
- 2 founder/behind-the-scenes videos
- 2 offer/product posts
- Daily stories during launch week

Content formats:

- Desk reset with Omah bracelet + tray + scent.
- Getting ready with necklace/bracelet.
- Nightstand routine with candle + roll-on.
- New home gifting kit.
- "What I gifted my friend who is starting over."
- "One small thing I wear before work."

### UGC Strategy

Use AI first to test hooks cheaply. Then send winning scripts to Indian creators.

Recommended creator volume:

- First month: 5 real creators
- Second month: 10-15 creators
- Third month: 20 creators if ads work

Expected cost:

- Low-cost creator videos: Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 5,000 per video
- Better creators: Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 15,000 per video
- Managed agency/content batch: Rs. 15,000 to Rs. 75,000 depending on volume

### Paid Ads

Do not scale ads early. Test small.

First test budget:

- Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 2,000 per day for 20-30 days
- Total first paid test: Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 50,000

Test only:

- 3 products
- 3 bundles
- 10 creatives
- 3 hooks per product

Kill rules:

- Kill ads with low add-to-cart.
- Kill products with no DMs or saves.
- Kill creatives after enough spend if they show no signal.

### WhatsApp Sales Funnel

Omah should use WhatsApp early because people will ask about meaning, gifting, and product selection.

Basic flow:

1. Instagram reel/comment.
2. DM automation or manual reply.
3. Send product/bundle link.
4. Ask occasion or feeling: self, gift, desk, sleep, protection.
5. Recommend one bundle.
6. Follow up after 24 hours.

## 9. Operational Plan

### Sourcing

Use Accio, Alibaba/IndiaMART, local jewellery vendors, candle/oil manufacturers, and packaging vendors.

Vendor scorecard:

- Landed cost
- MOQ
- Delivery time
- Quality consistency
- Customization ability
- Packaging support
- Return/replacement terms

### Inventory

Start with micro inventory.

Recommended first batch:

- 5 wearable SKUs x 30-50 units
- 3 scent/home SKUs x 30-50 units
- 3 kits x 20-30 units

Do not overstock before seeing demand.

### Packaging

Packaging matters because we are selling perceived value.

Must-haves:

- Pouch or box
- Ritual card
- Small care card
- Soft tissue/wrap
- Brand sticker
- Thank-you note

## 12. Final Recommendation

Omah should launch like a lean D2C brand, not like a full wellness company.

The smartest route is:

1. Start with wearable ritual jewellery because it is easy to buy, gift, ship, and film.
2. Add scent/home ritual products because they create repeat purchase and daily use.
3. Package everything into ritual gift kits because kits raise AOV and profitability.
4. Use creator videos and WhatsApp selling to learn quickly.
5. Add beauty-lite and teas only after the first drop proves demand.

Recommended budget: Rs. 4L to Rs. 7L over 60 days.  
Minimum workable budget: Rs. 1.5L to Rs. 2.5L over 45 days.  
Do not spend Rs. 10L+ until the first drop shows product-market pull.
